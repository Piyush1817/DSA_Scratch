%s: String
%d: Decimal integer (for byte, short, int, long)
%f: Floating-point number (for float, double)
%e: Exponential floating-point number (scientific notation)
%c: Character (for char)
%b: Boolean (true or false)
%o: Octal integer (base 8)
%x: Hexadecimal integer (base 16)
%t: Date/Time (requires an additional character to specify the format, e.g., %tY for the year)
%n: Platform-specific newline character
%%: A literal percent sign (%)
